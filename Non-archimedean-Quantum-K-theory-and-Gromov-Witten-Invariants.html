Non-Archimedean Quantum k-theory and Gromov-Witten Invariants

These notes were taken during the talk given by Tony Yue YU (Caltech)

Definition (Calabi-Yau variety):
A smooth projective variety X/CX/\mathbb{C}X/C is called Calabi-Yau if its canonical bundle KXK_XKX​ is trivial, i.e., it has nowhere vanishing holomorphic volume form.
Examples are elliptic curves, abelian varieties, K3 surfaces, hypersurface of degree d+1 in CPd\mathbb{CP^d}CPd

Mirror symmetry is conjectural duality between Calabi-Yau varieties:
Calabi-Yau variety XXX ⟷\longleftrightarrow⟷ ∃\exists∃ mirror variety X∨X^\veeX∨

such that geometric relations hold between XXX and X∨X^\veeX∨: Hodge structure, Gromov-Witten invariants, Fukaya categories, derived category of coherent sheaves, SY2 torus fibration, etc.
Further study reveals that mirror symmetry is actually a duality between “maximal degenerating family” of Calabi-Yau varieties.

Classical approach to Gromov-Witten theory is via Perfect obstruction theory (by Behrend-Fantechi, Li-Tian)
